Overview

StreamWave Entertainment is an advanced analytics project focused on resolving a core business challenge: strategic resource allocation in the media sector. By analyzing viewer engagement data, this system helps determine which genres drive long-term subscriber retention versus those that simply consume high production budgets with weak returns.

The solution features an interactive Streamlit dashboard integrated with an AI-powered assistant, allowing stakeholders to use natural language to uncover trends in viewing hours, completion rates, and genre-specific ROI.

Key Business Goals

  • Maximize ROI: Directing content spend toward genres like Drama and Comedy that deliver high sustained engagement.
  • Reduce Churn: Aligning content production with the 88% retention rate targets identified in the data.
  • Improve Engagement: Leveraging analytics to encourage longer viewing times (targeting 100 mins avg) and repeat consumption.
  • Support Marketing: Focusing campaigns on high-impact genres to gain a competitive edge in niche markets.

StreamWave Content Performance Report

Prepared by: Edwina B. Abam, Business Intelligence Specialist

1. Executive Summary

This analysis addresses the critical problem of content budget fragmentation. With viewer interests spread across many genres and increasing competition, StreamWave must invest confidently. Our findings show that while Drama has the highest reach, Comedy leads in repeat engagement. By optimizing these high-performing categories, we can reduce the risk of wasted spend and subscriber churn.

2. Genre Performance Deep Dive

Drama: The dominant force on the platform with the highest total viewing hours and strong repeat rates.

Comedy: Exceptional engagement metrics with a very high repeat rate, making it a "habit-forming" genre.

Sci-Fi & Action: High viewership potential; however, Action requires improved retention strategies to convert one-time viewers into loyal subscribers.

4. AI Intelligence Assistant

Stakeholders can interact with the data directly via the integrated chatbot to answer critical questions:

  • "Which genres drive the highest repeat viewing?"
  • "What is the completion rate for Documentary content?"
  • "How can we improve retention for Biography subscribers?"

5. Strategic Recommendations

Core Content Investment

Action: Allocate 60%+ of the production and marketing budget into Drama and Comedy to solidify the subscriber base.

Target: Maintain 88%+ Retention.

Niche Genre Refresh

Action: Refresh the Documentary format and introduce niche sub-genres in Sci-Fi to capture loyal audiences without "Big Audience Bias."

Target: Increase Sci-Fi completion rate by 15%.

Cross-Genre Collaboration

Action: Promote cross-genre initiatives (e.g., Action-Comedy hybrids) to migrate users from underperforming categories to high-value ones.

Target: Lower the churn of one-time viewers.

6. Conclusion

By shifting from a volume-based strategy to an engagement-based strategy, StreamWave can ensure that every dollar spent on content contributes directly to subscriber longevity. Investing in Drama and Comedy, while refreshing niche formats, provides the most stable path to long-term profitability.
