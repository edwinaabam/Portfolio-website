Predictive Modeling for Perishable Product Demand

Python Random Forest XGBoost Streamlit Generative AI (Gemini)

Overview

This project focuses on FrostMart UK, a leading national retail chain with over 800 stores. The primary objective was to transition the company from intuition-based to evidence-based inventory management using regression-based predictive modeling.

The Business Challenge: FrostMart faced a staggering £12.2M annual loss. This was split between £5.2M in overstocking wastage and £7M in lost revenue due to stockouts during peak seasonal periods.

Key Features

  • Dual-Model Intelligence: Integration of Random Forest and XGBoost for high-resolution forecasting.
  • Environmental Factoring: Model accounts for weather-driven demand spikes (30% of total volatility).
  • Executive Dashboard: A Streamlit interface designed for store managers to simulate "What-If" scenarios.
  • Generative AI Integration: Google Gemini-powered "Managerial Briefings" and natural language data queries.

Model Performance & Evaluation

I evaluated three distinct algorithms to identify the most robust solution. The ensemble methods (Random Forest and XGBoost) achieved near-perfect accuracy, dramatically outperforming the Linear baseline.

Generative AI Implementation

Leveraging Generative AI (Google Gemini), the project provides conversational intelligence. Managers can ask complex questions like "How will the predicted heatwave affect bakery stock in London?" and receive immediate, natural language advice.

Strategic Roadmap & Recommendations

1. Dynamic AI-Guided Replenishment

Action: Mandate the use of the new forecasting application for all daily and weekly ordering processes within the Bakery and fresh Meat categories.

Target: Reduce Bakery wastage from 14.7% to < 9.0% in 6 months.

2. Targeted London Region Initiative

Action: Establish a pilot program in London to fully integrate the AI forecasting tool with store-level operational workflows to address population density complexities.

Target: Decrease London wastage rate by 5% in 1 fiscal year.

3. Optimized Promotional Strategy

Action: Standardize the use of the 25% discount level for key promotional events, as it has proven to be the most effective at lifting sales (51.3% uplift).

Target: Increase sales uplift by 10% while reducing post-promo waste.

4. Marketing Investment Reallocation

Action: Shift a significant portion of the budget towards the first quarter (January to March), identified as the period of highest marketing effectiveness.

Target: Improve marketing efficiency by 20% in 12 months.

Conclusion

FrostMart UK is at a pivotal juncture. By embracing these data-driven changes, the company will reclaim millions in lost profit and solidify its position as a technologically advanced leader in the UK grocery sector.
